Day 1: Arrival in Kyoto

The train from Tokyo was remarkably quiet. Stepping onto the platform at Kyoto Station, the air felt different—thinner, cooler, and carrying the faint scent of incense and cedar. We dropped our bags at the Machiya near Higashiyama and set out immediately.

"In the heart of Kyoto, silence isn't the absence of sound, but the presence of focus."

Higashiyama Sunset

Walking up Sannenzaka at dusk was like stepping back in time. The wooden storefronts started lighting their paper lanterns. We found a small tea house that served Matcha from Uji. The bitterness was perfectly balanced by the wagashi.

  • Evening stroll through the Yasaka Shrine
